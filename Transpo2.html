What are Hydrone Subsea Drones?

Hydrone subsea drones, developed by Saipem's Sonsub center, are advanced robotic solutions for unmanned deep-water operations up to 3,000 meters.

Key features include:

  • Multi-Sector Applications: Offshore energy, renewable energy, infrastructure monitoring, fish farm support, and water quality assessment
  • Autonomous Operation: Inspection and intervention capabilities with digital seabed reconstruction
  • Sustainable: Reduces CO2 emissions by up to 90% compared to traditional methods
  • Hybrid Functionality: Combines ROV and AUV capabilities for versatile operations

Hydrone-R performing subsea operations

How Do They Work?

Hydrone docking at subsea station

The Hydrone drones operate through advanced autonomous technology:

  1. Hybrid Operation: Function as both ROVs and AUVs using AI and sensor technology
  2. Extended Deployment: Can operate for up to 12 months on the seabed with 12-hour work/recharge cycles
  3. Specialized Fleet:
    • FlatFish for inspections
    • Hydrone-R for combined inspection and intervention with robotic arms
    • Hydrone-W for heavy-duty operations like emergency valve closures

The system eliminates the need for vessel support through subsea docking stations, enabling continuous operations.

When Were They Introduced?

The Hydrone program has evolved through several key milestones:

  • 2015: Hydrone program launched by Saipem
  • 2018: FlatFish development began under Shell license
  • 2019: Hydrone-R trials commenced
  • 2019-2021: Commercialization phased rollout
  • 2024: Hydrone-R achieved 167 days continuous operation at Njord Field

The program continues to evolve with ongoing advancements in AI and electrification technologies.

Hydrone prototype testing

Where Are They Already Implemented?

Hydrone-R at Njord Field

Current implementation areas:

  • Njord Field, Norway: Hydrone-R deployed under 10-year contract with Equinor since 2019
  • Offshore Energy: Oil and gas field inspections and interventions
  • Renewable Energy: Testing for floating wind farm applications
  • Non-Energy Sectors: Aquaculture, defense, and environmental monitoring

The drones are planned for broader use in offshore energy, infrastructure monitoring, and environmental studies, supporting sustainable and cost-effective operations.
